About Me

I am a self-taught Web Developer based in Manchester, looking to break into the industry and develop my skills. My current knowledge favours (but is not limited to) frontend development; encompassing HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Redux & Styled Components.

I earned a Bachelors of Science in IT at Sheffield Hallam University, where I was first introduced to fundamental concepts in programming & software development.

I don't skimp on theory BUT I think programming is best learned through practically applying concepts. Most of what I know comes from many hours of my own research & experimentation, as well as online *project-based* courses & tutorials. Ultimately, I believe this hands-on approach has helped me to adopt a mindset for growth and gives me confidence in my abilities.

Determining how users will interact with a website/web app has spiked my interest in UI/UX design. The core of my passion for Web Development lies in the freedom it provides to exercise creativity alongside analytical thinking, two things that aren't often synonymous in other professions. For me, it's this juxtaposition and multifaceted nature of web development that maintains my interest - there is never a dull moment and always something to learn.

Discord is a popular online social platform with many programming communities; I will often turn to such channels when in need of help. When possible, I like to give back by working with others to solve their problems. This has taught me the value of teamwork, and is why I look forward to working with a team regularly (and with like-minded people in general).

At the moment, I am job-seeking and my main goal is to apply & level-up my frontend skills in real-world projects (whether it be JS, React or CSS). When the opportunity presents itself (in the workplace or in my own time), I aim to:

  • strengthen my understanding of the backend and available tools (e.g. Node.js, PostgreSQL or MongoDB)
  • expand my skillset to include a statically typed language (like TypeScript or C#)

When I'm not coding, I'm:

  • Playing guitar
  • Reading - Fiction, Biographies, Productivity
  • Running, skipping or weight training
  • Keeping up with Science & Tech - from Software Development, Computer Hardware and Gaming, to Psychology, Robotics, Astronomy & Space Travel
